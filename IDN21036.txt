Severe Thunderstorm Warning - Canberra
For people in Canberra and Queanbeyan.

Issued at 4:19 pm Tuesday, 19 December 2023.

Severe thunderstorms in Canberra have temporarily eased.

Severe thunderstorms in the warning area have temporarily eased. However, the redevelopment of severe thunderstorms remains possible. The situation is being closely monitored and further detailed warnings will be issued as necessary.

Canberra Airport recorded a wind gust of 87 km/h.

Gungahlin recorded hail around 2-3cm.

The State Emergency Service advises that people should:
    * Keep clear of fallen power lines. 
    * Keep clear of creeks and storm drains. 
    * Don't walk, ride your bike or drive through flood water. 
    * For emergency help in floods and storms, ring the SES (NSW and ACT) on 132 500. 
 

The next warning is due to be issued by 5:20 pm.

A more general severe thunderstorm warning is also current for the Hunter, Southern Tablelands, North West Slopes and Plains, South West Slopes, Australian Capital Territory and parts of the Mid North Coast, Illawarra, South Coast, Central Tablelands, Central West Slopes and Plains, Riverina, Lower Western, Upper Western, Snowy Mountains and Northern Tablelands districts.

Warnings are also available through TV and Radio broadcasts, the Bureau's website at www.bom.gov.au or call 1300 659 210. The Bureau and State Emergency Service would appreciate warnings being broadcast regularly.
